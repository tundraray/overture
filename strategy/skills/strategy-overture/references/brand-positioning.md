# Brand Positioning

## Perceptual Maps

A 2D visualization showing how customers perceive competing brands on key dimensions.

### Axis Selection

Choose the two most strategically relevant dimensions:

| Common Axis Pairs | Best For |
|-------------------|---------|
| Price vs. Quality | Commodity/premium positioning |
| Functional vs. Emotional | Product vs. lifestyle brands |
| Simple vs. Feature-Rich | UX-driven differentiation |
| Niche vs. Mass Market | Segment focus decisions |
| Innovation vs. Reliability | Tech product positioning |

### Building a Perceptual Map

```yaml
Step 1: Identify 6-10 competitors (direct + indirect)
Step 2: Choose 2 axes based on customer research
Step 3: Score each brand on both axes (1-10)
Step 4: Plot on 2D grid
Step 5: Identify clusters, gaps, and your position
```

### Interpretation

| Pattern | Meaning | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Cluster** | Many brands compete similarly | Differentiate or move away |
| **White space** | No brands occupy this quadrant | Potential opportunity (validate demand first) |
| **Isolation** | One brand alone in a quadrant | Strong position if demand exists; weak if no market |
| **Drift** | Your brand moving over time | Deliberate = strategy; accidental = brand erosion |

## Brand Positioning Statement

### Template

```
For [target audience]
who [statement of need or opportunity],
[brand name] is a [product category]
that [key benefit / promise].
Unlike [key competitor],
[brand name] [primary differentiator].
```

### Quality Criteria

| Criterion | Good | Bad |
|-----------|------|-----|
| **Specificity** | "For Series A SaaS founders scaling sales" | "For businesses" |
| **Differentiation** | "Unlike spreadsheet-based forecasting" | "Unlike competitors" |
| **Credibility** | Claims backed by evidence | Aspirational without proof |
| **Memorability** | Simple, distinct, repeatable | Complex, generic |

## Positioning Strategies

| Strategy | Description | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Category Leader** | Be the default in a known category | You're #1-2 in an established market |
| **Category Creator** | Define a new category | Blue Ocean strategy, no existing category fits |
| **Challenger** | Position against the leader | #3-5 with a genuine differentiation story |
| **Niche Specialist** | Own a specific sub-segment | Can't win broad market, strong in a vertical |
| **Value Player** | Same quality, lower price | Cost structure advantage, scale economics |

### Category Creation vs. Category Entry

```yaml
Create new category when:
  - Your product doesn't fit existing categories
  - Existing categories carry negative associations
  - You can define the evaluation criteria
  - Budget exists to educate the market

Enter existing category when:
  - Category has strong buyer intent (people search for it)
  - You can credibly compete
  - Education cost is low
  - Category is growing
```

## Brand Architecture (Multi-Product)

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Branded House** | Master brand on everything | Google (Maps, Drive, Docs) |
| **House of Brands** | Each product has own brand | P&G (Tide, Gillette, Pampers) |
| **Endorsed** | Sub-brands endorsed by master | Marriott (Courtyard by Marriott) |
| **Hybrid** | Mix based on strategic need | Microsoft (Office, Xbox, LinkedIn) |
