# Blue Ocean Strategy

## Core Concept

Create uncontested market spaces (blue oceans) instead of competing in crowded markets (red oceans). Pursue **value innovation** — simultaneous differentiation AND cost reduction.

| Red Ocean | Blue Ocean |
|-----------|-----------|
| Compete in existing market | Create uncontested market |
| Beat the competition | Make competition irrelevant |
| Exploit existing demand | Create and capture new demand |
| Value-cost trade-off | Break the value-cost trade-off |
| Align activities with differentiation OR cost | Align activities with differentiation AND cost |

## Strategy Canvas

A one-page visual comparing your offering against competitors across key factors.

### How to Build

1. **Identify competitive factors** — What does the industry compete on? (price, features, speed, support, brand, etc.)
2. **Rate each competitor** — Score each factor 1-5 for each competitor
3. **Plot your current curve** — Where do you stand today?
4. **Design your target curve** — Apply Four Actions Framework to reshape

### Template

```markdown
## Strategy Canvas: [Product/Industry]

### Competitive Factors
| Factor | Industry Avg | Competitor A | Competitor B | Our Current | Our Target |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| [Factor 1] | [1-5] | [1-5] | [1-5] | [1-5] | [1-5] |
| [Factor 2] | [1-5] | [1-5] | [1-5] | [1-5] | [1-5] |
| ... | | | | | |

### Value Curve Interpretation
- **Convergence zones**: Where all players look the same → opportunity to diverge
- **Over-serving zones**: Where industry invests but customers don't care → reduce
- **Under-serving zones**: Where customers want more but nobody delivers → raise/create
```

## Four Actions Framework

Apply to each competitive factor:

| Action | Question | Effect |
|--------|----------|--------|
| **Eliminate** | Which factors does the industry take for granted that should be eliminated? | Removes cost, simplifies |
| **Reduce** | Which factors should be reduced well below industry standard? | Cuts cost without losing core value |
| **Raise** | Which factors should be raised well above industry standard? | Creates differentiation |
| **Create** | Which factors should be created that the industry has never offered? | Opens new value space |

### Four Actions Template

```markdown
## Four Actions Analysis: [Product]

### ELIMINATE (factors to remove entirely)
1. [factor] — Rationale: [why customers don't need this]
   Cost savings: [estimate]

### REDUCE (factors to scale down)
1. [factor] — Reduce from [current] to [target]
   Rationale: [industry over-invests here]

### RAISE (factors to scale up)
1. [factor] — Raise from [current] to [target]
   Rationale: [customers value this more than industry delivers]

### CREATE (entirely new factors)
1. [factor] — Description: [what this would look like]
   Value unlock: [why customers care]
   Feasibility: [High / Medium / Low]
   Cost: [estimate]
```

## Six Paths Framework

Six systematic paths to discover blue oceans:

| Path | Direction | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Path 1**: Alternative industries | Look across industries solving same job | Netflix: entertainment, not just video rental |
| **Path 2**: Strategic groups | Look across tiers within industry | Lexus: luxury quality at premium (not luxury) price |
| **Path 3**: Buyer chain | Look at different buyers (user vs purchaser vs influencer) | Bloomberg: financial data for traders, not IT |
| **Path 4**: Complementary products | Look at what happens before, during, after your product | IKEA: entire home furnishing experience |
| **Path 5**: Functional-emotional appeal | Switch the orientation | Swatch: watch from functional to fashion |
| **Path 6**: Time | Shape external trends | Apple: foresaw digital music shift |

## When Blue Ocean Strategy Fails

| Failure Mode | Why | Mitigation |
|-------------|-----|-----------|
| Blue ocean exists but is tiny | Not every uncontested space is worth contesting | Validate market size before committing |
| Imitation destroys advantage | Blue ocean becomes red within 12-18 months | Build barriers: IP, network effects, switching costs |
| Execution gap | Great strategy, poor implementation | Include resource plan and timeline in canvas |
| Customer doesn't want what you created | "Create" factor based on assumption | Validate with customer research before full build |
