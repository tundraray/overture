# Business Model Canvas & Lean Canvas

## Business Model Canvas (Osterwalder)

Nine building blocks describing how a company creates, delivers, and captures value.

### The Nine Blocks

| Block | Key Question | Category |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Customer Segments** | Who are we creating value for? | Front-stage |
| **Value Propositions** | What value do we deliver? | Front-stage |
| **Channels** | How do we reach customers? | Front-stage |
| **Customer Relationships** | What relationship does each segment expect? | Front-stage |
| **Revenue Streams** | How do we capture value? | Front-stage |
| **Key Resources** | What assets are essential? | Back-stage |
| **Key Activities** | What must we do well? | Back-stage |
| **Key Partnerships** | Who are essential partners? | Back-stage |
| **Cost Structure** | What are the main costs? | Back-stage |

### BMC Template

```markdown
## Business Model Canvas: [Company/Product]

### Customer Segments
- **Primary**: [segment] — [size, characteristics]
- **Secondary**: [segment] — [size, characteristics]

### Value Propositions
- **For [segment 1]**: [value delivered]
- **For [segment 2]**: [value delivered]

### Channels
| Phase | Channel | Purpose |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Awareness | [channel] | [how they discover us] |
| Evaluation | [channel] | [how they evaluate us] |
| Purchase | [channel] | [how they buy] |
| Delivery | [channel] | [how they receive value] |
| After-sales | [channel] | [how we support] |

### Customer Relationships
| Segment | Relationship Type | Purpose |
|---------|------------------|---------|
| [segment] | [self-serve/dedicated/automated/community] | [acquisition/retention/upselling] |

### Revenue Streams
| Stream | Model | Pricing | % of Revenue |
|--------|-------|---------|-------------|
| [stream] | [subscription/usage/license/etc.] | $[X] | [X]% |

### Key Resources
- **Physical**: [assets]
- **Intellectual**: [IP, brand, data, partnerships]
- **Human**: [key roles/expertise]
- **Financial**: [capital requirements]

### Key Activities
- **Production**: [what we build/deliver]
- **Problem Solving**: [what we figure out]
- **Platform/Network**: [what we maintain]

### Key Partnerships
| Partner | Motivation | Key Activity |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| [partner] | [cost optimization/risk reduction/resource acquisition] | [what they provide] |

### Cost Structure
| Cost Category | Type | Amount | % of Total |
|--------------|------|--------|-----------|
| [category] | [fixed/variable] | $[X] | [X]% |
```

## Lean Canvas (Ash Maurya)

Startup-focused adaptation. Optimized for finding product-market fit under uncertainty.

### Differences from BMC

| BMC Block | Lean Canvas Replacement | Why |
|-----------|----------------------|-----|
| Key Partners | **Problem** | Startups need to validate problem first |
| Key Activities | **Solution** | Focus on what you're building |
| Key Resources | **Key Metrics** | Track progress with data |
| Customer Relationships | **Unfair Advantage** | What can't be easily copied |

### Lean Canvas Template

```markdown
## Lean Canvas: [Product]

### Problem (Top 3)
1. [problem] — Existing alternatives: [how they solve it now]
2. [problem] — Existing alternatives: [current workaround]
3. [problem] — Existing alternatives: [what they do instead]

### Customer Segments
- **Early Adopters**: [who will use this first, and why]
- **Mass Market**: [eventual target]

### Unique Value Proposition
**One-liner**: "[headline that captures your value]"
**High-level concept**: "[X for Y]" (e.g., "YouTube for courses")

### Solution (Top 3 Features)
1. [feature] → solves Problem #1
2. [feature] → solves Problem #2
3. [feature] → solves Problem #3

### Channels
- [channel 1]: [free/paid, expected CAC]
- [channel 2]: [free/paid, expected CAC]

### Revenue Streams
- [stream 1]: $[X]/mo per [unit]
- **Target ARPU**: $[X]/mo
- **Break-even**: [X] customers

### Cost Structure
- **Fixed**: $[X]/mo (team, infrastructure)
- **Variable**: $[X] per customer (support, compute)
- **Burn rate**: $[X]/mo
- **Runway**: [X months]

### Key Metrics (AARRR)
| Stage | Metric | Target |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Activation | [metric] | [target] |
| Retention | [metric] | [target] |
| Revenue | [metric] | [target] |

### Unfair Advantage
[Something that cannot be easily bought or copied]
Options: Insider info, expert endorsement, dream team,
personal authority, large network, community, existing
customers, SEO ranking, proprietary data
```

## When to Use Which

| Situation | Use | Why |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Pre-MVP, idea stage | Lean Canvas | Focus on risk and assumptions |
| Series A, scaling | BMC transition | Need to articulate full operations |
| Series B+, established | Full BMC | Complete business architecture |
| Pivot exploration | Lean Canvas | Rapid hypothesis testing |
| Investor communication | Both | Lean Canvas for pitch, BMC for due diligence |

## Canvas Evolution

Canvases are living documents:

```yaml
Iteration Triggers:
  - Customer interview reveals new insight
  - Experiment validates or invalidates assumption
  - Competitor makes significant move
  - Revenue model needs adjustment
  - New segment discovered

Update Cadence:
  - Pre-PMF: Every 2 weeks
  - Post-PMF: Monthly
  - Scaling: Quarterly
```
